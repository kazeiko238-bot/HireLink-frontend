HIRELINK PRIVACY POLICY

Last Updated: April 27, 2026

1. Introduction

HireLink ("we," "our," or "us") respects your right to privacy. This Privacy Policy outlines how we collect, protect, and process your personal data in accordance with Republic Act No. 10173, also known as the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (DPA) of the Philippines.

2. Information We Collect

To provide our multi-perspective evaluation services, we collect the following:

  • Personal Information: Name, address, email, phone number, and birthdate.
  • Sensitive Personal Information: Government-issued IDs (for verification), marital status, and educational background.
  • Professional Data: Resumes, work history, skills, and certifications.
  • Evaluation Data: Results from technical tests, behavioral assessments, and peer feedback gathered through the Platform.
  • Technical Data: IP addresses, browser types, and cookies to improve performance.

3. Purpose of Data Processing

We process your data for the following legitimate purposes:

  • To create and maintain your professional profile on HireLink.
  • To perform multi-perspective evaluations to assess candidate-employer fit.
  • To enable Employers to search, view, and contact potential candidates.
  • To improve our evaluation algorithms and platform security.
  • To comply with legal and regulatory requirements in the Philippines.

4. How We Share Your Information

With Employers: We share your professional profile and evaluation results with Employers only according to your selected privacy settings.

With Service Providers: We may share data with third-party vendors (e.g., cloud storage) who are contractually bound to protect your data.

Legal Necessity: We may disclose information if required by Philippine law or a valid order from the National Privacy Commission (NPC).

5. Data Retention

We retain your personal data for as long as your account is active. If you request account deletion, we will purge or anonymize your data within thirty (30) days, except for data required for tax or audit purposes.

6. Your Rights as a Data Subject

Under the DPA, you have the following rights:

Right to be Informed: To know how your data is being processed.

Right to Access: To request a copy of the personal data we hold.

Right to Rectification: To correct any errors in your info.

Right to Erasure: To request the removal of your data.

Right to Data Portability: To obtain your data in an electronic format.

7. Security Measures

We implement organizational, physical, and technical security measures—including end-to-end encryption and restricted access protocols—to protect your data against unauthorized access or disclosure.
